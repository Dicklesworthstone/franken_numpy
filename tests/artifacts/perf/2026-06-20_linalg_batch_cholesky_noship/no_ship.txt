## DISPROVEN hypothesis: per-lane allocation in batch_map_lanes.
Extended the direct-write path (cholesky_nxn_into_out, byte-identical, zero
per-lane alloc) from n<16 to n<CHOL_MID_MIN(128). Rebuilt + measured: NO
improvement (d=16 still 6.3x). Alloc/copy is NOT the bottleneck. REVERTED.

## TRUE root cause: scalar per-lane unblocked Cholesky kernel.
RAYON_NUM_THREADS=1 (serial) fnp is 6.3-8.6x slower than numpy:
  d=16 6.32x, d=32 7.80x, d=64 8.58x  (serial)
=> fnp's scalar triple-loop cholesky_nxn is ~6x slower PER LANE than LAPACK
   dpotrf (loop-carried dot-product reduction does not vectorize; numpy's
   gufunc calls tuned LAPACK per lane). Parallelism only partly compensates
   and is high-variance (d=16 ranged 1.6x-6.3x across runs) - memory-bandwidth
   / rayon-granularity bound, NOT achieving ~16x core scaling.

## Real lever (separate, larger effort): a SIMD/blocked per-lane Cholesky
kernel matching LAPACK dpotrf throughput, or batched panel factorization
across lanes (lane = SIMD vector). Must stay byte-identical to cholesky_nxn
golden. High-risk in contended fnp-linalg; not a wrapper/gate tweak.
